Future Art Markets

8/24/2017

1 Comment

 
Are you ready for the cryptocurrency art world? Well, that hurricane is building steam and it is ready to knock down some gallery walls. This news comes from the strong desire of creators getting credit and recognition for supplying the social world with free content that makes money for the big companies. You spend all your time and energy in your creative endeavors that provide content to the internet. You are just trying to get your creative business going. You just want to build recognition so that you can continue to fulfill your destiny of dedicating your life to creative endeavors. Cryptocurrency startups are gaining ground. Synereo, one of the startups, is in the beta stages of building their system which allows digital curators and content consumers to share and give "AMPs" which is the name of the cryptocurrency by Wildspark and Synereo. From what can be gathered, an Amp is equal to about $0.23. The system is advertised as a new way to "pay attention". You the consumer or curator would see content and use the Wildspark plugin to actively Amp up a piece of content. It is an interesting idea in that it gives people the power to validate the types of content they see on the internet. It is parallel voting with money. Of course this leaves quite a bit of room a collective mindset which excludes the very unique individuals which provide art and culture with its essence. In an ideal world this looks to be a nice system of acknowledging artists and creatives with actual currency. With that in mind, It would take a lot of generosity, time and acknowledgement to provide a real financial boost. However, this is a currency system. Will people just hoard the currency and just treat it as another selfish scheme to gain and leave the actual intended beneficiaries of the system out in the cold? It is easy to speculate. The point of this is to simply make aware that there are companies exploring the digital frontiers and possibilities that cryptocurrency has to offer. Cofound.it is a startup that works as a crowdfunding site, but with digital currency. They point to some new projects and startups such as Maecenas (a decentralized art gallery). Maecenas looks to provide access to the art world through a system of buying pieces of a work of art. Yes, exactly like it sounded, a piece of a masterpiece. It is like buying stock in a painting. Use cryptocurrency to own shares of a multi-million dollar Picasso or Warhol. This new system will also be translated to current works by artists. Like any gallery, artists and works are likely to be curated. On the other hand, this system could provide great potential to allow an artist to sell their work to the masses. This also has some potential to take hold with galleries across the country. So let me begin the trend by saying you can own a share of the digital work i made for this blog post. I am opening 1000 shares available at $1 a share. So, if you are an artist or content creator, keep creating and know that the world is ever evolving. You don't have to follow the system to succeed. However, It is new territory and it does provide opportunities. We live in a connected world in which we can buy a piece of the sky and now maybe someday even a piece of Mona Lisa's Smile.

Make a Splash

7/19/2017

0 Comments

 
The beautiful thing about being creative is that you get to remove boundaries of life. Being creative is being free. Nothing can ever take away what you have created. Even when and if you are the only one who sees your creation. It existed in a fleeting moment and you got to experience that contribution. So, if you must create, you should just let yourself make the biggest splash that you can. It can be a mental splash or physical exaltation. I know i don't post on this blog often, but an expectation that I should produce content consistently is inadequate. I am a maker of images. It often takes time to find those unique, original slices of my being. I work at a pace that suits my humanity. I am concerned with pushing my limits. So, please know that i still exist and i am busy finding the right colors, shapes, lines and textures that bring images with some origin of a creative species. Whether that strikes a chord with people is not the key. I look for little notes placed in the right moments which perk up the imagination. Wandering souls connect on the flowing winds, and time and time again they seek the clues which make the mysteries clear in their mind. Splash, Swoosh and Swim until you make the splash that makes the water reach the shore. It's you versus the water. Move the water.

Like a Sword from Stone

4/19/2017

2 Comments

 
What is it about the textural sense of materials that embed themselves upon the mind? Are we just curious to understand what something is so we feel secure in its existence? Is it just primal nature to need experience through something tangible? The need for expression is not endless, yet we long to experience when we are not expressing. Without one, it seems there is no fuel for the other. But if i were to just paint flames in your mind like so, then wouldn't it make sense? These questions are at the heart of the discussion of visual art. Otherwise, why ever stand endlessly in front of a painting, longing to figure out why you are there? So many days go by that we spend learning and experiencing our world, but how often do we spend expressing our reaction to the world and not to our own experience? It is never a fully conscious expression or we may never have a need to seek its understanding. The unique visual language we create is a product of expression. However, it is very important that you be your own guide in your experience and that you understand that the subconscious influence of the world be filtered through your senses and processed fully before your expression is to become an individualized and visual statement which reflects your inherent human qualities. We are all swords in stones. Not everyone can just lift us from our place. We are moldable only through the intense flames of our personal experience.

Becoming an Artist

12/30/2016

0 Comments

 
     To make a choice to be an artist is a decision that often seems to be an unforgiving life choice. Some people may constantly think you to be foolish. Well the pursuit of any career can be foolish. People spend lifetimes in a chosen career only to lose that certainty right before the end. Most Important is the fact that If the passion to create still follows you, then you must recognize it to be something of value. Whether you feel it is a higher calling or if it just provides you a sense of self, It is a necessity that humans do not ignore such a passionate opporunity to connect themselves to a stream of consciousness which allows for an exploration of personal experience. No one has the ability to tell you who you are meant to be. Certainly, you will feel the struggles of your obligations. But do you not also have an obligation to express your freedom of existence?
     With that being said, I believe that choosing to be an artist also puts pressure on you to be the best version of yourself. Staying organized, setting goals and accomplishing those goals are things which should be a consistent set of questions that must be answered constantly. The tricky thing is that you must always understand and allow time for these goals to be realized. Sometimes your goals will require time to come to fruition. Some of the greatest masterpieces have taken years, some decades. Realizing that we are humans that wander and doubt is a critical part of the process.
       Investing in making art often requires critical budgeting practices which effect the outcome of your work. It is absolutely critical to know what you will need to be able to finish any project before it is begun. Of course, this is not always possible. However, to be organized enough to help yourself establish your vision for the outcome is of great importance. Sometimes, only the visualization of the idea is enough to propel you forward to a great realization or breakthrough in your work. Is that not the ultimate goal? To continually move forward, improve upon and learn from your work? It is ok if that is not your purpose for creating art. Some simply meditate through creativity. Some make products for consumers. Some make products for collectors. Others may be searching for purpose or innovation.
     Whichever your intent of creating, the choice of becoming an artist is only different in that it requires an intense level of dedication for it is a choice of time, of investment and of great opportunity. Moments of assurance that the choice is justified should not be the focus, for the choice alone holds your personal acceptance of joining a larger historical conversation about humanity.
​      If you are an experienced artist at any level, I encourage you to share and post your thoughts in the comments, you may hold a key to someone's particular decisive moment.  I wish you all a Happy New Year in 2017!!!
